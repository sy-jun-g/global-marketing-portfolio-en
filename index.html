01
LinkedIn Channel Revival
Contribution: 100%
Paid Social Content Strategy ✦ LLM Tools Newsletter
Background

The company's LinkedIn page had been dormant for over a year — most followers were inactive, and posts were limited to occasional global event updates. The long absence of a dedicated global marketer had left the channel on autopilot.

Goal

Reactivate a "dead" page. Rather than jumping to lead generation, the immediate goal was a visible, measurable win: growing the follower count.

Approach

Targeted cybersecurity professionals in EMEA and the US with weekly curated industry news. Used LLM Tools to select trending topics, summarize news, and refine copy at speed. Supplemented with company updates in newsletter format.

Lesson Learned

Early ad setup was wrong — "follower objective" campaign drove CPA to KRW 30,000. Pivoted to content the audience actually wanted (weekly news), dropping CPA to KRW 2,000. Sometimes the best way to get what you want is to stop chasing it directly.

03
Gartner Analyst Relations
Contribution: 95%
Analyst Relations Stakeholder Comms B2B
Background

Global recognition in cybersecurity requires more than marketing — it requires the endorsement of authoritative decision-makers like Gartner analysts, whose reports directly influence enterprise buying decisions worldwide.

Goal

Establish a regular cadence of product briefings with Gartner analysts to build visibility toward inclusion in global cybersecurity reports.

Approach

Prepared for each meeting by studying product specs with PMs and deepening knowledge of cybersecurity, cloud, and data concepts beyond typical marketing scope. Led with genuine curiosity and respect — building rapport rather than pitching.

Lesson Learned

The goal was to get listed in a Gartner report. But the way to get there was to stop acting like it. Analysts respond to authenticity and expertise, not sales tactics. Learning to separate your objective from your behavior in the room was the real skill.
